How this is calculated

The calculator uses the standard future-value formula for a starting balance plus level monthly contributions, compounding monthly at your annual return divided by twelve.

Solving forWhat it finds
Monthly amountThe contribution needed so your starting balance plus deposits and growth reaches the goal in your timeframe.
TimelineHow many months your chosen contribution takes to reach the goal, given the return assumption.

Compound growth is whatever the ending balance exceeds your starting balance and total contributions. The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission's Investor.gov explains this compounding effect and stresses that past returns never guarantee future results, which is why the return here is an input you set, not a fixed figure.

Choosing a return assumption

The right assumption depends entirely on where the money lives. Money in cash or short-term savings has historically earned low single-digit rates, the FDIC publishes national deposit-rate averages you can check. Money invested in a diversified long-term portfolio has historically earned more over long periods, but with real risk of loss and no guarantee in any given year. When in doubt, a lower assumption produces a more conservative, safer plan. This tool does not recommend any account, product, or investment.

What this doesn't account for

  • Taxes on interest, dividends, or gains.
  • Account fees, which reduce real returns.
  • Inflation, a dollar in the future buys less than a dollar today.
  • Return variability, real markets move up and down rather than growing at a steady rate.

Common questions

What return should I assume?

Match it to where the money sits. Cash and short-term savings: low single digits (check FDIC national averages). Long-term diversified investing: historically more, but with risk and no guarantee. Lower assumptions make a safer plan.

How does compound growth change what I need?

Growth does part of the work, more so the longer money sits. Short-term goals are mostly your own contributions; long-term goals lean more on growth. The split is shown in the result.

What does this assume?

Monthly compounding, level end-of-month contributions, and no taxes, fees, or inflation. It's a planning estimate, not a projection of guaranteed value.
